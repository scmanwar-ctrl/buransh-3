You Are Not Mine

And I want to say that first, clearly and without hesitation.
Not because it hurts to accept, but because truth deserves respect.

I cannot call you mine, and I never try to.
Your life, your heart, your story — they belong exactly where they already are.
This note isn't an attempt to step into that space.
It isn't a question, a request, or a hope disguised as words.

It is simply honesty.
I love you — quietly, carefully, and without expectation.
The kind of love that does not ask to be chosen,
the kind that understands boundaries and still finds meaning in admiration.
To me, you are like a sunflower.
A sunflower doesn't claim the sun,
yet it turns toward it every day without complaint.
It doesn't compete for light,
doesn't ask for attention,
it just blooms — honestly and fully.

That's how you exist.
There is something about the way you are —
the way you smile without trying,
the way your presence softens a moment,
the way silence feels less heavy when you're near.
You don't arrive loudly,
yet your absence is always noticeable.
If I were to count the good in you,
I would lose track.
There are a hundred things — maybe more —
the small kindnesses, the calm you carry,
the warmth that follows you without you realizing it.
And no matter how closely I look,
I don't find a single thing to call a flaw.
Not because you are flawless,
but because everything about you feels human in the most beautiful way.
Some people don't need balance between good and bad —
they are simply good.

I know your happiness is tied to a life that isn't mine to touch.
And I accept that — fully.
There is no resentment here, no sadness meant to be shared.
Only respect.

This love doesn't want to interfere.
It doesn't want answers or reassurance.
It doesn't need to be returned.
It only exists because you exist.

If this note ever reaches you,
I hope it feels light — not heavy.
I hope it feels like appreciation, not pressure.
And if you ever choose to forget these words,
that's okay too.

Wherever you are,
whoever you laugh with,
whatever path you continue on —
I hope you keep blooming.
Bright, warm, and honest.
Like a sunflower.
Always facing the light.
Asking nothing in return.

— with nothing but respect
